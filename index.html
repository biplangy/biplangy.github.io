My name is Biplang and I create conservation pathways for our shared future with plants and animals. I currently work for the Te Whangai Trust, providing impact assessments and carbon sequestration estimates for their forest restoration projects.

My PhD research at the University of Canterbury involved analysing the behaviour and efficacy of African pouched rats (Cricetomys sp. nov) in dispersing seeds for germination. This work enabled me to develop skills in experimental design, wild-life recording, live-capture trapping, and geo-spatial data analysis (with SECR Density and QGIS).

I am currently open to career opportunities that contribute to the conservation of biological resources in New Zealand. If you would like to know whether I am a good fit for your mission, please do get in touch.
