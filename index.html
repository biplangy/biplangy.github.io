My PhD research at the University of Canterbury focused on the behaviour of African pouched rats (Cricetomys sp. nov) in relation to their seed dispersal potential in a West African montane forest. Alongside previous research experiences, this has sharpened my skills in surveying wildlife and woody plants, in conducting data analyses using R and QGIS, as well as in science communication.

I am open to any biology-related or multi-disciplinary collaborative opportunities. So, if you would like to know whether I am a good fit for your mission, please do get in touch.
